Classification Legend

N — Normal Sinus Rhythm
A healthy, regular heartbeat controlled by the heart’s natural pacemaker (sinus node). Rate is typically 60–100 bpm at rest.
S — Supraventricular Ectopic
An extra or premature beat originating from the upper chambers (atria). Often feels like a skipped beat or flutter. Usually benign but may need monitoring if frequent.
V — Ventricular Ectopic
An abnormal beat from the lower chambers (ventricles). Appears as a wide, unusual complex on ECG. Occasional ones are common; frequent ones need clinical review.
F — Fusion Beat
A “hybrid” beat where a normal and ventricular impulse merge, producing a mixed shape. May indicate co-existing ventricular ectopy.
Q — Unknown / Paced
A pacemaker-generated beat (sharp spike before QRS) or an unclassifiable beat. Paced beats are expected in patients with implanted devices.
